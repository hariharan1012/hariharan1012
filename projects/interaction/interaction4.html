Project Overview

Problem Statement

The client, an Indian luxury goods company, wanted an application that will aid their Human Resource team manage and empower their associates. The existing application was clustered with information would often confuse the user on what they were looking at. This lead to information misinterpretation which directly impacted the associates. The client wanted a solution that would enable users help maintain and empower associates and reduce exit percent.

My Approach

To understand the problem statement, my team and I connected with the client for a walkthrough session on the existing application. We then performed a deeper analysis on the application to identify KPI’s and optimal journey for a user to achieve their task. After several discussions, we were able to identify the prime KPI’s and defined our approach solution. Post a soft sign off, we started our design phase.

The Design

As the users tasks were defined, we requested access to sample data that would help us understand on the various impacts and magnitude of usage the application would have. On receiving the data, we analyzed and conceptualized different approaches on how we could represent the data. Post several discussions within our team we decided on best approach concept and begun detailed design of the application.

Discovery Phase

The Workflow

For this process, we spoke to some of the users and also got a walkthrough of the existing application. This helped us analyze, identify and prioritize the KPI’s. To ensure our understanding was inline with the client, we created an initial concept design of our idea and presented to the client. Our goal was to ensure that the users were able to access all the information they require with as less as clicks possible without impacting the performance of the application.

Learnings

RE-USE IF POSSIBLE

The requirement may have been for a redesign, but spotting components that clearly communicate their intention is essential. A designer should always try to retain some sense of familiarity in their design to help users understand the new system as fast as possible with very little learning curve, therefore, it is not wrong to reuse components.
