Getting Started

Being a UX Designer in the service industry and design thinking paving the way for all businesses, clients deals is a major task of proving that I belong in the Consulting business. Over the last two plus years I have been associated with Cognizant, I have actively worked on many proposals and won several of them including new logos, representing my competency. My key strategy is to listen to the expectations and tailor our services around it.

My Approach

Usually, I request a connect with the client to understand their expectations. Once the stage has been set, analyzing the requirements takes a mojor role. During this phase, I list out the tasks that to be performed in the application. On identifying the tasks, I envision a high-level view of what the screen might look like. With these components in place I provide an estimate on timeline and resources the project might take to be accomplished.

Upselling

Some requirements need the extra mile to secure the project. In these cases, I prepare a deck taking a sample use case and put forth the design thinking process: creating user persona, defining their journey and a sample screen on how the screen might look based on my assumptions. The second part of the deck would bring in information on how we, as a service, would be a strategic and ideal partner for the client. This part will highlight on how we provide an end-to-end service and how we leverage design thinking.

CLIENT MEETINGS

I have been involved in developing the proposals as well as the client pitch materials. I represent the UX competency and bring my experience to bear to help win the project. Through the sales process, I explain how our approach and method to UX and how our team and services bring value to their business. I have often been an integral part of the winning pitch team - enabling us win significant programs of work by bringing to bear our collective and individual experiences in UX and the development life-cycle.
